Menstrual Phase
Days 1–7 · Release · Intuition · Renewal
Root-Cause Lens — Aviva Romm, MD Estrogen and progesterone are at their lowest. The uterine lining sheds, inflammatory mediators rise temporarily, and the nervous system becomes more sensitive. In TCM, this is governed by the Chong Mai (Penetrating Vessel) and requires free-flowing Liver Qi, sufficient Kidney Jing, and abundant Blood. For those on a conception journey, Romm notes that the quality of your bleed is a vital sign: bright red flow for 3–5 days with no clots is optimal. Brown, clotted, scanty, or very painful bleeds signal Blood stagnation or deficiency — root causes she directly links to subfertility and poor endometrial quality.
Source: Hormone Intelligence, Ch. 2–4, 12, 15 — Aviva Romm MD

✦ Fertility lens — what your bleed tells you (Romm, Ch. 3 & 15)
  • Flow quality is a vital sign. Bright red, no clots, 3–5 days = optimal. Brown, clotted, scanty, or very painful bleeds signal Blood stagnation or deficiency — root causes Romm directly links to poor endometrial quality and subfertility.
  • Severe cramping (dysmenorrhoea) warrants investigation for endometriosis or adenomyosis. Romm notes these are addressable at the root — not just managed with NSAIDs.
  • Short bleed (<3 days) or very light flow may signal thin uterine lining — the "soil" the embryo implants into. Romm's protocol: nourish Blood, support Kidney Yin, reduce overexercise.
  • This phase sets the foundation for the entire cycle. The quality of your rest, nourishment, and nervous system repair here shapes egg quality over the coming weeks.
  • Romm recommends testing: full thyroid panel (TSH, FT3, FT4, TPO antibodies), iron studies & ferritin, CBC, Day 3 FSH/LH/estradiol/AMH — ideally in early follicular phase.
  • Romm: "Your body is not a lemon. It is far from broken. What is broken is our food system, our environment, our healthcare system." Your symptoms are messages, not flaws.

Follicular Phase
Days 6–14 · Emergence · Possibility · Growth
Root-Cause Lens — Aviva Romm, MD FSH stimulates follicle development and estradiol rises, rebuilding the uterine lining. Romm describes this as the Yin phase in TCM — Kidney Yin and Blood are nourished, Essence (Jing) rises. She emphasises that follicle development begins approximately 90 days before ovulation, meaning what you eat, sleep, and experience now (and over the past 3 months) directly determines egg quality. Romm is explicit: a high omega-6 to omega-3 ratio, insulin resistance, and chronic inflammation — all driven by diet and lifestyle — are primary root causes of ovulatory dysfunction and subfertility.
Source: Hormone Intelligence, Ch. 2, 6, 11, 15 — Aviva Romm MD

✦ Fertility lens — egg quality & the 90-day window (Romm, Ch. 11 & 15)
  • Romm is clear: follicle development begins ~90 days before ovulation. What you do now — and have done over the past 3 months — directly determines egg quality. This is a long game of nourishment, not a quick fix.
  • Estrogen dominance signs (heavy periods, breast tenderness, bloating, short cycles) indicate impaired liver estrogen metabolism. Root-cause approach: cruciferous vegetables, DIM/I3C, reduce alcohol and dairy, heal gut microbiome (Romm, Ch. 6 & 10).
  • Thin uterine lining (<7mm at ovulation): focus on Kidney Yin nourishment, acupuncture, adequate healthy fats and iron, and avoid over-exercising which diverts blood flow away from the uterus.
  • PCOS pattern: Romm identifies insulin resistance as the primary root cause in 50–70% of PCOS cases. Protocol: low-GI whole food diet, myo-inositol, eliminate sugar and ultra-processed foods, address stress and sleep (Romm, Ch. 13).
  • Romm recommends: Day 3 bloods (FSH, LH, estradiol, AMH) — best taken in early follicular phase to assess ovarian reserve.
  • Thyroid: Romm frequently identifies undiagnosed or sub-optimally treated hypothyroidism as a hidden cause of anovulation and miscarriage. TSH ideally <2.5 for conception. Always test TPO antibodies (Romm, Ch. 2 & 15).

Ovulation Phase
Days 14–16 · Peak · Expression · Connection
Root-Cause Lens — Aviva Romm, MD The LH surge triggers egg release — the pivot from Yin to Yang in TCM. Romm explains that the egg is viable for only 12–24 hours, but sperm can survive 3–5 days in fertile (egg-white) cervical mucus — making the 5 days before ovulation equally important. Anovulation (not ovulating) is the single most common cause of female infertility, and Romm identifies its primary root causes as PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhea (from overexercise, stress, or under-eating), and thyroid dysfunction — all addressable at the root.
Source: Hormone Intelligence, Ch. 2, 3, 7, 13, 15 — Aviva Romm MD

✦ Fertility lens — your conception window (Romm, Ch. 3 & 15)
  • Romm's "cycle sense": egg-white cervical mucus is your most reliable free fertility sign — it keeps sperm alive and guides them to the egg. Learn this sign above all others.
  • OPK peak (LH surge) = ovulation ~24–36 hours away. Romm recommends timing intimacy the day of peak OPK and the following day, alongside learning mucus signs.
  • BBT: a sustained rise of >0.2°C confirms ovulation occurred. Track over 3+ cycles to find your true pattern. No rise = possible anovulation — investigate.

  • After ovulation: Romm is specific — shift immediately to warmth, rest, and nourishment. Avoid vigorous exercise, very cold food/drinks, and high stress during the implantation window (days 6–10 post-ovulation).
  • Consider: Day 21 (or 7 days post-ovulation) progesterone blood test — Romm identifies this as the most important fertility marker in the luteal phase, confirming ovulation occurred and assessing corpus luteum function.

Luteal Phase
Days 17–28 · Integration · Nesting · Completion
Root-Cause Lens — Aviva Romm, MD Progesterone rises from the corpus luteum — this is the Kidney Yang phase in TCM, warming and transforming. Romm emphasises that progesterone is profoundly stress-sensitive: the HPA axis (cortisol system) directly competes with progesterone production. "Higher levels of stress hormones may double infertility risk," she notes (Ch. 15). Low progesterone (luteal phase defect) — shown as cycles shorter than 10 days, premenstrual spotting, or Day 21 progesterone below 30 nmol/L — is a direct barrier to implantation. Romm also clarifies that PMS is not normal: "Severe PMS is always a sign of hormonal imbalance, most commonly estrogen dominance relative to low progesterone, or disrupted serotonin/dopamine" (Ch. 12).
Source: Hormone Intelligence, Ch. 2, 7, 12, 15 — Aviva Romm MD

✦ Fertility lens — implantation window & luteal support (Romm, Ch. 12 & 15)
  • Days 6–10 post-ovulation is the implantation window. Romm's protocol: warmth, rest, low stress, nourishment. Avoid intense exercise, fasting, cold exposure, and alcohol completely during this window.
  • Luteal phase defect (LPD): phase shorter than 10 days, or Day 21 progesterone below 30 nmol/L, indicates inadequate corpus luteum function — a direct barrier to implantation. Romm's root-cause approach: Vitex, B6, stress reduction, adequate sleep, and address PCOS or thyroid issues (Ch. 15).
  • Romm on PMS: "PMS is not normal — it is a sign of hormonal imbalance." Severe mood shifts, breast tenderness, bloating, and cramping are signals of estrogen dominance or low progesterone — both addressable at the root. "You deserve to feel well every day of your cycle" (Ch. 12).
  • Premenstrual spotting (brown blood before period starts): often signals low progesterone or Kidney Yang deficiency. Vitex, B6, and progesterone-supporting herbs can help significantly (Romm, Ch. 12 & 15).
  • The two-week wait: Romm acknowledges the anxiety is understandable, but notes cortisol actively suppresses progesterone. "I ask my patients to shift focus from getting pregnant to optimising health" — and in many cases, stepping back from trying so hard leads to conception (Ch. 15).
  • Romm recommends: DUTCH hormone test to map your full hormonal picture across the cycle if PMS is severe, cycles are irregular, or implantation continues to fail. This reveals estrogen metabolism pathways, cortisol patterns, and progesterone levels across the cycle.
