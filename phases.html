Menstrual Phase
Days 1–7 · Release · Intuition · Renewal
TCM & Root-Cause Lens In TCM this phase is governed by the Chong Mai (Penetrating Vessel) and the downward movement of Blood. Healthy menstruation requires free-flowing Liver Qi, sufficient Kidney Jing, and abundant Blood. For those on a conception journey, a difficult or incomplete bleed often signals Blood deficiency, Liver Qi stagnation, or Cold in the uterus — all addressable through food, herbs, acupuncture, and lifestyle.

✦ Fertility lens — what this phase tells you
  • Flow quality matters: bright red, no clots, 3–5 days is optimal. Brown, clotted, scanty or very painful bleeds signal Blood stagnation or deficiency — key root causes in subfertility.
  • Severe cramping (dysmenorrhoea) often indicates endometriosis, adenomyosis or Cold in the uterus — worth investigating functionally.
  • Short bleed (<3 days) or very light flow may indicate thin uterine lining — the "soil" that the embryo implants into.
  • This is your body clearing and preparing. Rest here deeply. The quality of this phase sets the foundation for the whole cycle.
  • Consider: full thyroid panel (TSH, FT3, FT4, TPO antibodies), iron studies, ferritin, CBC at this time of cycle.

Follicular Phase
Days 6–14 · Emergence · Possibility · Growth
TCM & Root-Cause Lens Post-bleed, the body begins rebuilding. In TCM this is the Yin phase — Kidney Yin and Blood are nourished, the uterine lining thickens, and Essence (Jing) rises. Functionally, estrogen rises and FSH stimulates follicle development. For conception, the quality of this phase directly determines egg quality and endometrial receptivity. Nourishing Yin deeply here is one of the most powerful things a woman on a fertility journey can do.

✦ Fertility lens — your most important phase for egg quality
  • Follicle development begins ~90 days before ovulation — what you do now (and over 3 months) directly influences egg quality. This is not a quick fix; it is deep nourishment.
  • Estrogen dominance signs (heavy periods, breast tenderness, bloating) often indicate Liver Qi stagnation and impaired estrogen metabolism — support with cruciferous veg, DIM, and liver herbs.

  • PCOS pattern (follicles not maturing): seed cycling, inositol, low-GI diet, and addressing insulin resistance are key root causes.
  • Consider: Day 3 bloods (FSH, LH, estradiol, AMH) — best taken in early follicular phase.

Ovulation Phase
Days 14–16 · Peak · Expression · Connection
TCM & Root-Cause Lens Ovulation is the pivot point — the transition from Yin to Yang, from building to warming. In TCM, the LH surge triggers the Kidney Yang to rise and the egg to be released. This requires strong Kidney Qi, free-flowing Liver Qi (to allow release), and abundant Jing. Anovulation or poor ovulation quality is often rooted in Kidney Yang deficiency, Liver Qi stagnation, or Phlegm-Damp obstruction (common in PCOS). This is the window of conception.

✦ Fertility lens — your conception window
  • The fertile window is typically 5 days before ovulation + the day of. Egg-white cervical mucus is your most reliable free sign — it keeps sperm alive and guides them.
  • OPK peak = LH surge = ovulation ~24–36 hours away. Time intimacy the day of peak and the day after.
  • BBT: temperature rises >0.2°C and stays elevated confirms ovulation occurred. Track over several cycles to find your true pattern.
  • No confirmed BBT rise or LH surge despite trying? Possible anovulation — worth investigating with a Day 21 progesterone blood test and potentially ultrasound monitoring.
  • Short luteal phase (<10 days from ovulation to period): suggests low progesterone — directly impacts implantation success. Vitex, Vit B6, and progesterone-supporting herbs help.
  • After ovulation: shift to warmth, rest, and nourishment. Avoid vigorous exercise, very cold food, and high stress during the implantation window (days 6–10 post-ovulation).

Luteal Phase
Days 17–28 · Integration · Nesting · Completion
TCM & Root-Cause Lens The luteal phase is governed by Kidney Yang — the warming, transforming energy that sustains the corpus luteum and maintains progesterone. For conception, this phase must be warm, long enough (minimum 10 days), and progesterone must be sufficient to hold an implanting embryo. In TCM, luteal phase defect is seen as Kidney Yang deficiency, sometimes combined with Liver Qi stagnation. This is also when the nervous system is most sensitive — unprocessed emotions, poor sleep, and high stress directly suppress progesterone.

✦ Fertility lens — the implantation window & luteal support
  • Days 6–10 post-ovulation is the implantation window. Prioritise: warmth, rest, nourishment, low stress. This is not the time for intense exercise, fasting, or cold exposure.
  • Luteal phase defect (LPD): phase shorter than 10 days, or Day 21 progesterone below 30 nmol/L suggests inadequate corpus luteum function — a direct barrier to implantation.
  • PMS is not normal — it is a sign of hormonal imbalance (often estrogen dominance relative to low progesterone, or low serotonin/dopamine). Severe PMS is addressable at the root.
  • Spotting before period (premenstrual spotting): often low progesterone or Kidney Yang deficiency in TCM. Vitex, B6, and progesterone support can help significantly.
  • PMDD or severe mood shifts: consider DUTCH hormone testing to map your full hormonal picture across the cycle.
  • The two-week wait: anxiety is understandable but cortisol actively suppresses progesterone. Practices that regulate the nervous system (breathwork, gentle yoga, acupuncture, meditation) are genuinely therapeutic here.
  • Consider: Day 21 (or 7 days post-ovulation) progesterone blood test — the most important fertility marker in this phase.
